Of a kind

Tucked away in Norwalk, Little Tokyo has been a beloved secret for over fifteen years. Chef Henry runs the kitchen with a passion that finds its way into every plate — sushi crafted with intention, sashimi that speaks for itself, and off-menu specialties prepared for those who know to ask.

The seafood arrives fresh from the New York Seaport daily. The menu reads like a love letter to Japan: silky uni, buttery unagi, handrolls assembled to order, and a steak & lobster that arrives to the table still steaming.

This is not a restaurant that performs tradition. It lives it.

The Experience

Small room.
Big soul.

Little Tokyo is not designed to impress — it impresses anyway. The room is intimate. The tables are close. The kitchen is open, and Henry moves through it with the ease of someone who has done this, joyfully, for decades.

Regulars call it a gem. First-timers call their friends before the bill arrives. Those who've lived in Japan say it's the closest thing this side of the Pacific.
